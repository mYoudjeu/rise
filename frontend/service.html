The RiseUp Foundation supports projects in favor of the emancipation of needy women and men, in Cameroon and around the world, carried out by RIseUP Foundation

Subsidy applications are submitted entirely online, and only within the framework of the Foundation's calls for projects. The selection procedure and criteria are detailed in the guidelines defined for each call for projects.

Context

Women are key actors in development, and their empowerment benefits everyone. They are, however, the first victims of inequalities, discrimination and violence in the world. Acting in favor of equality between women and men means promoting economic prosperity, political stability throughout the world, ecological transition and contributing to the emergence of a more inclusive society. In order to promote the empowerment and improvement of women's living conditions, a foundation is created, under the aegis of IUC, the RiseUP Fondation.

The Foundation contributes to the achievement of the Sustainable Development Goals defined by the United Nations, and more particularly Goal No. 5 which aims for gender equality and the empowerment of all women and girls on the horizon 2035. The Foundation is launching a new call for projects to financially support associative projects in favor of the emancipation of women and men, community projects.

What are the projects targeted by the call for projects?


Through this call for projects, the RiseUP Foundation wishes to provide financial support for projects carried out by French associations which aim to promote the empowerment of women, equal rights between women and men, and then improving the living conditions of girls and women. Projects aimed at particularly vulnerable populations – exiled women, homeless women, disabled women, or single mothers – are given priority. These projects can take place in Cameroon, abroad or have a multi-country dimension. Specific attention is paid to projects taking place in overseas departments and regions, in northen cameoon, northwest and southwest cameroon. Finally, projects must fall within one or more of the Foundation's 4 areas of intervention:

Fight against violence against women and girls: The Foundation defends the rights of women and fights against discrimination and the physical, sexual or psychological violence of which they are victims. Through the projects it supports, it combats attacks on the integrity and dignity of women through prevention, support and reconstruction.

Education and leadership of women and girls: In order to facilitate girls' access to school and to encourage women's leadership, the Foundation supports projects aimed at combating the exclusions and discrimination of which women are victims, promoting access to essential social services and thus creating favorable conditions for the realization of their aspirations.

Professional integration and economic rights: The Foundation supports associations that support women in gaining financial autonomy. It allows them to be independent, to meet the needs of their families and to integrate into society, by accessing vocational training, employment or creating their own business.

Women's actions for the environment: The Foundation supports projects that promote women's actions in the protection of the environment and the fight against climate change: projects for sustainable agriculture, management of natural resources, management of waste, renewable energy production – involving women. It pays specific attention to projects that present innovative approaches and adaptation strategies allowing the preservation of natural resources and biodiversity.

Eligibility criteria



The project must contribute to the emancipation of women and girls especially ,to men and to the improvement of their living conditions, to the promotion of equal rights.

Only one file can be submitted to the Foundation per organization and per year (excluding Prizes organized by the Foundation). Organizations already supported by the Foundation (project currently in progress) cannot submit a new application.

a student with an innovative project having the capacity to reduce unemployment rate and catalyzing economic growth can propose his project

Selection procedure


The selection of projects is carried out in several phases:

Phase 1: Preliminary steps

Project leaders are invited to ensure the eligibility of their application by consulting the guidelines for calls for projects. Submission of grant applications is done entirely online. Projects sent by post or email will not be considered.

Phase 2: Submission and review of grant application forms

Project leaders are invited to complete a short grant application form online, accessible only when a call for projects is in progress. The grant application forms are then reviewed by the Foundation's team, which verifies that the proposals are eligible and shortlists those that best meet the Foundation's objectives and priorities.

Phase 3: Submission and examination of grant application files

Shortlisted organizations then have one month to complete a complete grant application file online. Once completed, the grant application files are subject to in-depth examination, aimed at assessing the solidity of the organization and the project, as well as their adequacy with the Foundation's criteria. Following the investigation, the team presents the projects to the Executive Committee of the Foundation.

Phase 4: Decision of the Executive Committee of the Foundation

The Executive Committee of the Foundation examines the projects pre-selected by the team and selects those which will be supported by the Foundation.

Commitment of associations supported by the Foundation



Project monitoring and evaluation Any project leader supported by the Foundation undertakes to comply with the planned monitoring-evaluation procedure, in particular by sending activity reports and financial statements regularly.

Communication Any project leader supported by the Foundation undertakes to mention the Foundation's support on the documents concerning the project. He undertakes to provide the Foundation with the documents (texts, photos, videos, etc.) to ensure the promotion of the project and the partnership in the Foundation's communication media.
